Their Humble Origins

It all started in the suburbs where 4 friends decided to do some hip hop music. Rock inspired the band to pick up certain instruments. Matt Helder picked up the drums, Jamie Cook picked up the guitar and Alex Turner took position of front man which he initially didn't want. Note that Andy Nicholson was in the band at this moment rather than Nick O'Malley. Jamie wanted to be in a band called the "Arctic Monkeys" and the name just stuck. The band started writing songs in Alex's garage and moved to performing in pubs by 2003. They made a 18 song demo, and gave it away anywhere they could. When a local photographer uploaded the band onto his website and giving the demo a name of Beneath the Boardwalk. This led to Arctic Monkeys being one of the first few bands to become popular due to the internet. Soon, the band released their debut single called "Five minutes with Arctic Monkeys" which made many labels want to sign them. However, they went with Domino Records as they were in love with the owner's idea of "Do it yourself" and soon released their first album called "Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not".

Favourite worst nightmare

Favourite nightmare was released with a few of the all time classics "505", "Brianstorm", "Flourescent Adolescent" and this album was the first album they recorded with their new bassist 'Nick O'Malley'. This was the start of the band's journey to find different kind of sounds to integrate into their music.

AM

AM was one of the most iconic albums ever and one of the most popular as this was when they changed how the songs sounded and how the lyrics were different from their old songs. Alex Turner picked up a persona and all of them were now these people who wore leather jackets and were cool. Their songs were now songs that could be blasted in their cars and a song to somewhat relate to.

Tranquility Base Hotel & Casino

This album was not famous mainly for the music but rather the reactions of fans towards this album. This album was a first step in a creative direction for the band where the song lyrics were more direct and the songs melody were different as they decided to evolve with the times. This album was their response to the everchanging music industry and fans would have to either listen or don't. This album was also the pedestal for the band to reach greater heights.
