Their Humble Origins

It all started in the suburbs where 4 friends decided to do some hip hop music. Rock inspired the band to pick up certain instruments. Matt Helder picked up the drums, Jamie Cook picked up the guitar and Alex Turner took position of front man which he initially didn't want. Note that Andy Nicholson was in the band at this moment rather than Nick O'Malley. Jamie wanted to be in a band called the "Arctic Monkeys" and the name just stuck. The band started writing songs in Alex's garage and moved to performing in pubs by 2003. They made a 18 song demo, and gave it away anywhere they could. When a local photographer uploaded the band onto his website and giving the demo a name of Beneath the Boardwalk. This led to Arctic Monkeys being one of the first few bands to become popular due to the internet. Soon, the band released their debut single called "Five minutes with Arctic Monkeys" which made many labels want to sign them. However, they went with Domino Records as they were in love with the owner's idea of "Do it yourself" and soon released their album called "Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not".
